About Our Story

El Restaurante was founded in 1962 by Isaías and Lucia Manzanares out their front window with a $2 tip and a dream, as Isaías' old story goes. Isaías took that very first order and commitment to authentic Mexican cuisine and built up and out from there, investing in new and improved equipment while keeping his same location.

The first notable renovation took place in 1965 when Isaías widened his front window and set up three tables in front of his house. Over time, the family both inside and outside the walls grew with crowded sidewalks on holidays and the Manzanares' son Gabriel being born in 1971.

After over ten years of becoming reknowned for in-home cooking, the Manzanares family broke ground at our current site in 1973. Construction finished within the year, not least because Lucia would visit the crew with lunch once a week. The grand opening at the start of the next year saw "a full house and a line all the way down the block" as reported in the Omaha World Herald.

Since then, we've enlarged our space two more times. Gabriel took over from his father in 2002 with the same words of gratitude that Isaías gave his very first customer. With a growing family of staff, a new online presence, and a likely expansion into Bellevue on the horizon, we would be glad to share our tastes with you.
